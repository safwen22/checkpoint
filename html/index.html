Porshe's history

Porsche history dates back to 1948, where founder Ferdinand Porsche created the Porsche brand with just 200 workers. The first Porsche model was introduced later that year, named the Porsche 356, and 52 cars were then produced in 1949 in a small garage. Today, when Bay Area drivers think of the Porsche brand, they think of luxury and high performing vehicles and rightly so. Porsche now produces many different models that deliver strong engine performance and offer a variety of unique comfort and convenience features that distinguish the Porsche brand.

Classic cars

    Launched: 1948

    Claim to fame:The Porsche that tops the list is in fact the iconic German carmaker’s first production vehicle.

  2. Porsche 959

    Launched: 1986

    Claim to fame: The Porsche 959 is the company’s first supercar produced between 1986 and 1989.

  3. Porsche 911 (993)

    Launched: 1994

    Claim to fame: The final incarnation of the air-cooled 911 first introduced in 1964, the 993-generation of the iconic 911 is considered by hardcore Porsche purists as the last truly great model in its long bloodline

  4. Porsche 911 Carrera RS

    Launched: 1973

    Claim to fame: The Carrera RS models are sometimes considered by the Porsche enthusiasts to be the most “classic” 911s of all time.
